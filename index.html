    Problem Statement

    Today's digital age, the market for social apps is over saturated with an abundance of platforms that encourage hookup culture and shallow connections. Unfortunately, this emphasis can impede individuals from finding authentic friendships within their respective communities, leaving them feeling disconnected and unfulfilled.

    Millennials, who spearheaded the social media era, are increasingly reliant on online sources to meet and connect. However, this shift has also led to a rise in social anxiety and isolation, making it challenging for them to form meaningful friendships and a sense of community – especially when online engagement is often superficial.

    “A poll of about 1,200 people by YouGov found 27% of Millennials have no close friends, and 22% report they have no friends at all. Most people find it difficult to make friends because they’re shy (53%), but people also report friendship is too much work (20%) or they are too busy (14%).” Forbes (2021)”

    Solution

    BuddySystem is a unique platform that strives to create a welcoming environment for everyone. With an "all-inclusive" approach, the platform is designed to facilitate authentic friendships and vibrant communities through engaging events and shared interests.

    User Persona: Friendly Frankie
    Column2
    Friendly Frankie is a new entrepreneur; working freelance at her own web design agency. Her career change keeps her busier than ever, with an unpredictable schedule. Her social circle is fairly small. She tends to spend most of her down time with her partner, but is craving platonic friendships. Residing in Toronto, her cost of living has been on a steady incline, so she’s taken up growing her own herbs on her condo balcony in hopes of saving a little money. She’d love to develop more of a green thumb and connect with others who could share their expertise.
